Research Focus

  • Work with Drosophila melanogaster to elucidate the mechanisms of adaptation using Insecticide Resistance
  • Development and analysis of xQTL (AKA Bulk Segregant Analysis) to identify genomic patterns of adaptation
  • Novel Methods development at the intersection of statistics, genomics & phenomics
  • Passionate about translational precision medicine and incorporation of multiomic data to study and predict biological processes
  • Applying for Computational Biology PhD programs in FALL 2027!

Current Works

Understanding the genetic basis of complex traits using population genomics and quantitative genetics.

Insecticide resistance evolution in Drosophila using pool-seq and xQTL experimental designs.

Thinking about the integration of machine learning techniques with traditional population genetic approaches to decrease noise and improve inference.

Methods development at the intersection of Bayesian statistics and high-dimensional genomic data.

Future Interests

Understanding the disease phenotype and its central dogma underpinnings.

Incorporating multiomic data to predict phenotypes and disease outcomes.

Translational precision medicine — from population-level signal to clinical insight.

Developing methods at the boundary of novel data generation (biology) and toolsets for its analysis (computation).
